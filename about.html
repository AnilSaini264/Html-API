Three generations. One sweet obsession.

What started as a grandmother's kitchen recipe is now a beloved neighborhood bakery — still hand-mixed, still slow-baked, still made with love.

A bakery built on butter and patience

In 2008, Isabella opened a tiny storefront with three recipes from her grandmother's worn cookbook. No marketing, no flashy packaging — just a sign on the door and the smell of warm vanilla drifting onto Rosewood Lane.

Sixteen years later, we still mix every batch by hand, still use real butter and real fruit, and still believe a cake should make you stop, smile, and reach for another slice
